# OWASP Top 10: Insecure Design — Mid / Senior Level

1. How do you embed threat modeling into design? — Use DFDs and STRIDE in planning.
2. How do you design for abuse resistance? — Add quotas, rate limits, and anomaly checks.
3. How do you handle insecure workflows? — Implement server-side state machines.
4. How do you enforce secure defaults across services? — Shared templates and policy-as-code.
5. How do you apply zero trust in architecture? — Verify every request and segment networks.
6. How do you design for multi-tenant isolation? — Tenant scoping and data partitioning.
7. How do you manage security exceptions? — Document and time-box risk acceptance.
8. How do you ensure privacy by design? — Minimize data collection and retention.
9. How do you design logging for investigations? — Structured, consistent, and correlated logs.
10. How do you plan for secure integrations? — Define contracts and validation at boundaries.
11. How do you handle high-risk actions? — Step-up auth and approvals.
12. How do you use security requirements in agile? — Convert to user stories with acceptance tests.
13. How do you prevent insecure feature flags? — Gate with auth and monitor usage.
14. How do you design safe file handling? — Quarantine, scan, and store out of web root.
15. How do you reduce attack surface? — Disable unused features and endpoints.
